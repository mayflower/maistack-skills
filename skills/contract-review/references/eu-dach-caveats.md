# EU / DACH Caveats

This skill is not a substitute for local legal advice. Use these cautions to avoid overconfident US-style recommendations when the agreement or parties point to EU or DACH law.

## Escalate or Qualify Recommendations When

- governing law or venue is Germany, Austria, Switzerland, or another EU member state,
- the agreement includes employee restrictive covenants,
- the paper is mainly about personal-data processing,
- mandatory statutory protections may override contract wording,
- the transaction involves works councils, employee transfers, or regulated procurement.

## Specific Watchpoints

- Employee non-competes and post-termination restraints often need narrower scope and separate enforceability analysis.
- Boilerplate governing-law suggestions from US practice may not be neutral or realistic for a DACH counterparty.
- DPAs, SCCs, TOMs, and cross-border transfer language should be reviewed with `gdpr-dsgvo-expert`.
- Consumer-style remedies, unfair-terms controls, and mandatory notice periods may matter even in mixed commercial settings.

## Drafting Guidance

- Prefer "flag for local counsel" over definitive enforceability claims.
- Separate commercial leverage advice from legal-enforceability advice.
- Keep negotiation asks practical: narrower scope, clearer standards, explicit process, better notice, more balanced termination, and stronger data governance.
