# NDA Review Notes

Use this reference for mutual or one-way NDAs, confidentiality agreements, teaser NDAs, and diligence access agreements.

## Focus Areas

- Directionality: one-way or mutual obligations
- Definition scope: avoid "all information" without standard carve-outs
- Survival term: 2-3 years for ordinary business information is more typical than 5+ years
- Residuals clause: decide whether unaided-memory use is acceptable
- Hidden restrictive covenants: non-solicit, non-compete, standstill, no-contact
- Compelled disclosure: notice and protective-order mechanics
- Return/destruction: timing, certification, backup carve-outs
- Assignment and disclosure to affiliates or representatives

## Common Red Flags

- No standard exceptions for public, prior-known, independently developed, or third-party information
- One-way confidentiality on what is described as a mutual discussion
- Broad non-solicit language embedded in an NDA
- Indefinite confidentiality for all information rather than just trade secrets
- Pre-agreed injunctive relief or bond waiver without reciprocal balance

## Typical Redline Directions

- Narrow the confidentiality definition with standard exceptions
- Reduce survival for non-trade-secret information
- Delete non-solicit language or narrow it to active solicitation of specifically covered contacts
- Add compelled-disclosure notice and reasonable cooperation language
- Clarify that legal retention and backup copies do not breach destruction obligations
